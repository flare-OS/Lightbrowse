Privacy Policy for LightBrowse
Effective Date: May 25, 2026

Welcome to LightBrowse, a privacy-focused web browser built using the Internet Explorer rendering engine.

Your privacy is a core principle of LightBrowse. By default, LightBrowse does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal data.

1. No Data Collection by Default
LightBrowse does not collect:
- Personal information
- Browsing history
- Search queries
- Device identifiers
- IP addresses
- Usage analytics
- Crash reports
- Telemetry data

2. No Telemetry
LightBrowse contains no telemetry systems or hidden analytics.

We do not track how you use the browser.

3. Downloads
LightBrowse supports file downloads.

Downloaded files are stored locally on your device only. LightBrowse does not upload, scan, or transmit downloaded files to our servers.

4. Local Browser Data
To function normally, LightBrowse may store data only on your device, including:
- Cookies from websites you visit
- Cached files
- Download history
- Saved settings/preferences
- Bookmarks (if supported)

This data remains local and is not transmitted to us.

5. Security Features
LightBrowse may include optional security protections designed to help identify unsafe or malicious websites.

These protections operate locally whenever possible and do not send your browsing history to us.

If a future version of LightBrowse ever requires limited data processing for security purposes, users will be clearly informed and asked for consent where required.

6. Third-Party Websites
Websites visited through LightBrowse may collect data under their own privacy policies. LightBrowse is not responsible for third-party data practices.

7. Children's Privacy
LightBrowse does not knowingly collect personal information from children—or any users—because it does not collect user data.

8. Changes to This Policy
If our privacy practices ever change, this Privacy Policy will be updated before those changes take effect.
